First problem statement and definitions

Suppose You are given coefficients \(a_0, a_1, \dots, a_n\) and \(b_0, b_1, \dots, b_m\) of two polynominals \(A(x), B(x)\), \(1 \le n,m,a_i,b_i \le 10^5\), and Your aim is to multiply them; in other words, You have to find such sequence \(c_0, c_1, \dots, c_{n+m}\) such that \((a_0+a_1x+a_2x^2+\dots+a_nx^n)(b_0+b_1x+b_2x^2+\dots+b_mx^m)=(c_0+c_1x+c_2x^2+\dots+c_{m+n}x^{m+n})=C(x)\). This problem can be solved in at least \(2\) versions: when You have to calculate coefficients as they are, or \(\pmod x\), where \(x\) has a primitive root and \(\exists_k 2^k|\phi(x) \land 2^k \ge m+n\)

First solution

One can multiply each element in brackets with element from second bracket, then add resulting \(nm\) elements; this solution works in \(O(nm)\)

Threshold of transformation

  1. If \(n+m=k\), then let \(w\) be minimal number, such that \(w \ge k \land \exists_{l \in Z} w=2^l\). Then, the result will be calculated as \(w\) coefficients, and polynominals \(A(x), B(x)\) will be used as if they had \(w\) coefficients (The additional coefficients will be evaluated to \(0\))
  2. One may change the methodology of obtaining a polynominal a little bit: instead of directly finding the coefficients, one may find \(w\) values \(x_i\), obtain values of both \(A(x_i)\) and \(B(x_i)\) in those points, then multiply values \(A(x_i)B(x_i)=C(x_i)=y_i\), and transform \(w\) values of polynominal \(C(x)\) in points \(x_i: C(x_i)=y_i\) into its coefficients - this process is called interpolation of a polynominal.
  3. Let \(A(x)=a_0+a_1x+ \dots + a_{w-1}x^{w-1}\)
    \(A_1(x)=a_1+a_3x+a_5x^2+ \dots a_{w-1}x^{\frac{w}{2}-1}\)
    \(A_0(x)=a_0+a_2x+a_4x^2+ \dots a_{w-2}x^{\frac{w}{2}}\)     What follows, \(A(x)=A_0(x^2)+xA_1(x^2)\)
